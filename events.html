November 1990

Dave Matthews records his songs for the first time with producer Steve Lillywhite

Early 1991

DMB records first in-studio demo titled "Remember Two Things" with original band members Peter Griesar and Boyd Tinsley

April 21, 1991

The band performs its first public show at a bar called Trax in Charlottesville, Virginia

September 27, 1994

DMB releases debut studio album "Under the Table and Dreaming" including hits like "What Would You Say" and "Ants Marching"

April 30, 1996

DMB releases their second studio album "Crash" including hits like "Crash Into Me" and "Too Much"

February 1996

The band wins its first Grammy Award for their song "So Much to Say" from the "Crash" album

October 28, 1997

DMB releases their first full-length live album "Live at Red Rocks 8.15.95" featuring performances from their 1995 summer tour

April 28, 1998

The band releases their third studio album "Before These Crowded Streets" including hits like "Don't Drink the Water" and "Crush"

Labor Day 2004

Dave Matthews Band performs their first annual Labor Dave Weekend shows, marking the beginning of a yearly tradition where the bland plays multiple nights over the Labor Day weekend in at the Gorge Amphitheatre in Washington state
